Case Study 02 · EcoFoote

EcoFoote
Sustainability Behavior App

Designed an MVP to help users measure, reduce, and be rewarded for sustainable behavior — backed by generative research across 177 survey respondents.

EcoFoote
UX Designer
Climate Technology
2020
1 PM · 6 UX · 3 Developers

The Challenge

EcoFoote connects people with sustainable businesses through a web application that takes users on a journey of measuring, reducing their carbon footprint, and rewarding sustainable behavior.

Building an MVP to attract investors and business partners were the main goals. But before jumping to solutions, we needed to understand users deeply.

"Sustainable behavior is an option — a choice that people have to make every day." — Key research insight

Research

Card Sorting — Finding Real Themes

Used card sorting to deduce broad themes from existing survey responses before designing anything:

  • Diet, Education & Awareness, Rewards
  • Transportation, Plastic Usage, Activism
  • Measuring Impact, Behavior Change

Survey Data — 177 Respondents

Finding Data
Wanted to reduce plastic in their lives 70%+
Found it inconvenient to recycle 35%

User Interviews — Three Behavioral Groups

Group 1 — Often engage in sustainable behavior. Motivated, need reinforcement.

Group 2 — Engage sometimes. Need lower-friction entry points and visible impact.

Group 3 — Rarely engage. Need education, awareness, and accessible first actions.

"I am trying to be intentional about the way I interact with the environment." — Tamra, Eco-Lodge Founder

"I know there is information on labels on how to recycle but I don't want to spend the time reading them." — Yboa, Business Analyst

Design Strategy

1 — Rewarding Sustainable Behavior

Users upload receipts, pictures, or documents related to sustainable actions to earn points — redeemable for gift cards, online shopping, or premium memberships.

2 — Activism as an Accessible Option

Users browse sustainability articles and choose to take action relevant to what they read — earning rewards for their activism.

3 — Measuring Impact to Drive Behavior

A carbon footprint calculator converts user actions into a score — giving users a concrete sense of how much impact their behavioral changes create.

4 — Business Directory Integration

Connects users with sustainable local businesses — enabling points to be earned through shopping choices as well as behavioral actions.

Outcome

One complete concept was presented to the EcoFoote founders — demonstrating the full user journey from onboarding through action, reward, and impact measurement. The most valuable lesson: sustainability is a genuinely complex, misinformation-laden topic that required constant stakeholder communication to navigate.
